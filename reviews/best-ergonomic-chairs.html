Ergonomic Chair Buying Guide

1. Lumbar Support — The Most Important Feature

80% of lower back pain from sitting is caused by inadequate lumbar support. The lumbar spine has a natural inward curve (lordosis) that flattens when you sit in a poorly designed chair. Good lumbar support maintains this curve. Look for: adjustable lumbar height, adjustable lumbar depth, and ideally independent sacral/lumbar support (like the Aeron's PostureFit SL).

2. Seat Depth — Often Ignored, Always Important

If the seat is too long, you'll either slouch or push your back away from the lumbar support. Proper seat depth means you can sit with your back touching the lumbar and have 2–3 fingers of space between the seat edge and the back of your knees. Most quality ergonomic chairs have seat depth adjustment — insist on this feature.

3. Armrests — 4D vs. 2D

2D armrests only go up and down. 4D armrests adjust height, width, forward/back, and pivot — letting you place your arms in exactly the right position to eliminate shoulder tension. For anyone who types for hours daily, 4D armrests are not optional.

4. Mesh vs. Foam — Which Is Better?

Mesh backs (Aeron, Leap) allow airflow and keep you cool. They also conform to your body shape dynamically. Foam backs feel more "plush" initially but compress over time and can feel hot. For long daily use, mesh generally wins. For cold climates or people who prefer the feel of foam, high-density foam (like in the Embody) can work well if the foam quality is high.
