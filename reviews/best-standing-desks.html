How We Test Standing Desks

Every desk in this guide was physically purchased, assembled, and used for at least 3 weeks before being included. Here's exactly what we measure:

📏
Stability Testing

We measure desk wobble at the lowest, middle, and highest positions — with and without weight loads. Using a smartphone accelerometer app, we get objective measurements.

🔊
Noise Measurement

Motor noise is measured with a calibrated decibel meter placed 3 feet from the desk during height adjustment. Under 50dB is our benchmark for "quiet."

⏱️
Assembly Time

We time assembly from box opening to first use with one person. We note tool quality, instruction clarity, and whether cable management is included.

🏋️
Load Testing

We test with 50 lbs (typical dual-monitor setup), 150 lbs, and near the rated maximum. We check if the motor struggles or if the frame shows stress.

Standing Desk Buying Guide — What Actually Matters

1. Height Range — Critical for Your Health

The most important spec nobody talks about. Your standing desk must reach the right height for your body. When standing, your elbows should be at a 90° angle with your hands on the keyboard. For most people this is 40"–44". Desks with 23"–52" ranges accommodate virtually everyone from 5'0" to 6'6".

2. Weight Capacity — Future-Proof Your Setup

A typical dual-monitor setup with laptop dock weighs 30–50 lbs. Triple monitors can hit 80 lbs. Add desk accessories and you're at 100 lbs easily. We recommend a desk rated for at least 200 lbs — even if you're only putting 50 lbs on it, more capacity = stiffer frame = less wobble.

3. Warranty — This Is Your Insurance Policy

Cheap desks have cheap warranties. A 2-year warranty tells you the company doesn't expect it to last. The Uplift V2's 15-year warranty is a statement of confidence. A longer warranty also means the company will be around to honor it.

4. Single Motor vs Dual Motor

Single motor desks work but are slower and wobble more at the top of their range. Dual motors split the load, move faster (typically 1.5–2" per second), and produce less wobble. For any desk over $500, dual motors should be standard.
