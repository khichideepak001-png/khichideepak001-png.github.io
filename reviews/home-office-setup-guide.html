Step 1: Choose Your Desk 🖥️

Your desk is the foundation of your setup. We strongly recommend a motorized height-adjustable standing desk — the ability to alternate between sitting and standing is the single biggest improvement you can make for your long-term health and energy.

How to Choose the Right Desk Size

Width: 48" minimum for a single monitor. 60"–72" for dual monitors. 80" for triple monitors or video production. Depth: 24" is the minimum. 30" is recommended — it puts your monitor at the ideal 20"–28" viewing distance automatically.

Correct Chair Setup (Do This First)

  1. Sit with your feet flat on the floor (or use a footrest)
  2. Adjust seat height so your knees are at 90°
  3. Adjust lumbar so it touches your lower back comfortably
  4. Set armrests so your elbows rest naturally without shoulder shrugging
  5. Check that your hips are slightly higher than your knees

Step 3: Monitor Positioning 📺

Incorrect monitor height is the #1 cause of neck strain. The fix is simple but critical.

👁️
Height

The top of your monitor should be at or just below eye level. If you find yourself looking down significantly, raise the monitor. A monitor arm makes this easy to adjust.

📏
Distance

20"–28" from your eyes. A simple test: extend your arm — your fingertips should nearly touch the screen. Most monitors are placed too close.

📐
Tilt

Tilt the screen back 10–20°. This reduces glare and keeps your neck in a more natural position. Avoid tilting forward.

Step 4: Keyboard & Mouse 🖱️

Your keyboard and mouse position affects your shoulder, wrist, and forearm health. Key rules:

  • Keep keyboard and mouse on the same level
  • Wrists should be straight (neutral) when typing — not bent up or down
  • Consider a wrist rest to maintain neutral wrist position
  • A split/ergonomic keyboard dramatically reduces RSI risk for heavy typists
